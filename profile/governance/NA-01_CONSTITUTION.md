## Preamble

Nyuchi Africa (Private) Limited exists to build **frontier
infrastructure** for Africa's unique economies. The company's
purpose is not an application, not a platform, and not a market.
It is the infrastructure itself — the data layers, networking
protocols, graph engines, identity primitives, and cryptographic
foundations that apps and platforms are built on.

The apps and platforms operated by the company — the Mukoko
consumer super app, the seven Nyuchi Enterprise products, and the
sister brands — are proof-of-concept and production expressions of
this infrastructure. They solve real problems for real communities
today. They also demonstrate, validate, and harden the
infrastructure for the decades ahead.

The company operates under a permanent symbiotic relationship with
the Mukoko Foundation, which governs the community's sovereign
assets. This Constitution describes how Nyuchi Africa governs
itself: how decisions are made, how work is organised, how
intellectual property is assigned, and how the company remains
faithful to its founding purpose as it grows.

The document is written for the current reality of the company —
pre-revenue, pre-scale, Founder-led — while establishing the
structures that will carry the company through growth without
requiring a constitutional rewrite at every inflection point.

## Part 1 — Identity and Purpose

### Article 1 — Legal Identity

1.1 Nyuchi Africa (Private) Limited is a private limited company
incorporated under the Companies and Other Business Entities Act
2019 of Zimbabwe, with registration number
9281/2019 and registered office at
3 Straven Court, 4 Browning Drive, Strathaven, Harare, Harare,
Zimbabwe.

1.2 The company trades publicly as **Nyuchi Web Services** for its
development hub and technical identity, and as **Nyuchi Africa**
for its corporate identity. The word *nyuchi* is Shona for *bee* —
a deliberate reference to collective intelligence, distributed
labour, and the pollination of an ecosystem larger than any single
actor within it.

1.3 The company operates under the three-way voting shareholding
lock described in Part 2 of the Symbiotic Charter: the Founder in
personal capacity, the Fawcett Family Holding Company, and the
Mukoko Foundation. No provision of this Constitution may be
interpreted inconsistently with the Symbiotic Charter.

### Article 2 — Purpose

2.1 Nyuchi Africa is a **frontier infrastructure company**. Its
founding purpose is to build and operate open-source and
community-based digital infrastructure that spans the frontier
domains of contemporary and emerging computing:

- **Web2 → Web3 → quantum.** The transition across today's web,
  the decentralised web, and the quantum era.
- **On-device, local-first, edge.** Compute and storage that work
  where the people are — resilient under low connectivity, private
  by default, ready for the AGI era.
- **Community-governed.** Platforms shaped by Ubuntu philosophy —
  collaboration, transparency, and shared ownership over extraction.

2.2 The company pursues this purpose across three divisions,
described in Part 3 of this Constitution. Revenue, apps, and
platforms are means, not ends. They sustain the infrastructure,
fund the team, prove the technology in the field, and fulfil the
company's obligations to the Foundation.

2.3 The company does not pursue revenue in ways that compromise
the Ubuntu purpose, the sovereignty mandate, the frontier
orientation, or the immutable boundaries of the Symbiotic Charter.

### Article 3 — Values

The company's values are not aspirational. They are operational
constraints on every decision the company makes.

**(a) Ubuntu.** Decisions are evaluated against their effect on
the community, not only their effect on the company. The
community's long-term benefit outweighs the company's short-term
convenience.

**(b) Sovereignty.** Speed is rented; truth is owned. Managed
services and proprietary infrastructure are used as ephemeral
caches and accelerants, never as sources of truth. Every component
of the platform has a sovereign fallback that runs in production,
not on paper.

**(c) Openness.** Public by default, private only when necessary.
Where a component must be private — principally, paid client
engagements — the choice is documented and justified. The burden
of proof falls on closure, not on openness.

**(d) Frontier orientation.** The company builds for decades, not
quarters. Post-quantum migration paths, local-first defaults, and
edge-native architectures are treated as present design
constraints, not future worries. When a choice is between the
comfortable path and the frontier path, the company takes the
frontier path — deliberately and with eyes open.

**(e) Honesty.** The company communicates its licensing, data
practices, sovereignty limits, and governance structures
accurately. Where a component is not fully sovereign — for example,
a source-available licence in a dependency — the company says so
plainly and documents its mitigation.

## Part 2 — Governance Structure

### Article 4 — The Founder & CEO

4.1 The Founder & CEO of Nyuchi Africa is **Bryan Fawcett**. The
Founder holds the office of CEO and serves as a Director of the
company.

4.2 At the current stage of the company's development, the Founder
exercises direct operational authority over all three divisions
described in Part 3. The company operates in a flat structure —
there are no formal division heads, and the Founder directs
development, platform, and parent-company work personally,
assisted by the team.

4.3 This flat structure is a pre-scale reality, not a permanent
design. Part 3 describes how the company will transition to a
divisional structure with formal division heads when specific
scale thresholds are reached.

4.4 The Founder's authority is bounded by:

- **(a)** The Symbiotic Charter and the Foundation's authority
  over the community's sovereign assets.
- **(b)** The three-way voting shareholding lock — any structural
  change requires unanimous consent of the Founder, the Family
  Holding Company, and the Foundation.
- **(c)** This Constitution, which the Founder may amend subject
  to Article 17.
- **(d)** Applicable law in Zimbabwe and in every jurisdiction in
  which the company operates.

### Article 5 — The Board of Directors

5.1 The company has a Board of Directors constituted in accordance
with its articles of association. The Board includes at minimum:

- **(a)** The Founder & CEO.
- **(b)** One Director nominated by the Family Holding Company.
- **(c)** One Director nominated by the Mukoko Foundation.

5.2 Additional Directors may be appointed by ordinary resolution
of the voting shareholders. Investor-nominated observer seats
(without voting rights) may be created in share subscription
agreements subject to the constraints of the Symbiotic Charter.

5.3 The Board meets at minimum four times per year, or more
frequently as convened by the Founder or by any two Directors. Its
responsibilities are those of a Zimbabwe private company board:
approval of annual accounts, declaration of dividends, appointment
of auditors, and oversight of material risk.

5.4 The Board does not direct day-to-day operations. Operational
authority rests with the Founder & CEO under Article 4. The
Board's role is oversight, not management.

### Article 6 — The Founder's Decision Rights

6.1 While the company operates in the flat structure described in
Article 4.2, the Founder exercises all of the following decision
rights directly:

- **(a)** Product and infrastructure roadmap and prioritisation
  across all three divisions.
- **(b)** Architectural decisions, including the canonical
  documents (Mukoko Order, Architecture, Manifesto) and all
  amendments to them.
- **(c)** Frontier-technology bets — which post-quantum algorithms
  to adopt, which local-first primitives to build on, which edge
  architectures to standardise on.
- **(d)** Hiring, role definition, and compensation for all
  employees and contractors.
- **(e)** Client engagement decisions for the development division.
- **(f)** Financial decisions within the annual operating budget
  approved by the Board.
- **(g)** Open-source licensing decisions, subject to the defaults
  in NA-02 (Open Source & Contribution Governance).
- **(h)** Partnership, integration, and commercial agreement
  decisions below the Board materiality threshold defined in
  Article 7.

6.2 The Founder may delegate any of these decision rights to team
members on a case-by-case basis, and will do so increasingly as
the team grows. Delegation is by written instruction (documented
in a decision log) and is revocable.

### Article 7 — Decisions Reserved to the Board

7.1 The following decisions are reserved to the Board and may not
be taken by the Founder alone:

- **(a)** Approval of the annual operating budget and material
  deviations from it.
- **(b)** Approval of the annual accounts and financial statements.
- **(c)** Any borrowing or credit facility above
  `USD 50,000` in aggregate outstanding balance.
- **(d)** Any capital expenditure above `USD 100,000` in a
  single commitment.
- **(e)** Any commercial agreement with an expected value above
  `USD 100,000` over its full term.
- **(f)** Any decision to enter or exit a country market.
- **(g)** Any change to the company's auditors or Compliance
  Officer.
- **(h)** Any decision material enough to require shareholder
  consent under the Symbiotic Charter or the articles of
  association.

7.2 The thresholds stated in Article 7.1 are the **initial
operative thresholds** adopted with this Constitution. They are
confirmed or adjusted by the Board at its first meeting after
adoption and reviewed annually thereafter. Until the Board acts
under this Article, the values stated in Article 7.1 apply.

### Article 8 — Decisions Reserved to the Shareholders

8.1 In accordance with the Symbiotic Charter and the articles of
association, the following decisions require the unanimous written
consent of all three voting shareholders (the Founder, the Family
Holding Company, and the Foundation):

- **(a)** Amendment of the articles of association.
- **(b)** Merger, acquisition, or disposal of the company or any
  material part of it.
- **(c)** Transfer, licensing, or encumbrance of the Nyuchi
  Honeycomb protocol, the Nyuchi Console, the Nyuchi Transfer
  Layer (NTL), SiafuDB, or any other core infrastructure asset.
- **(d)** Appointment or removal of the Foundation-nominated
  Director.
- **(e)** Winding up or dissolution of the company.
- **(f)** Issuance of new voting shares or conversion of
  non-voting shares to voting shares.
- **(g)** Any amendment to the Symbiotic Charter itself.

## Part 3 — Organisational Structure

### Article 9 — The Three Divisions

9.1 The company organises its work around three divisions, each
with a distinct role in the ecosystem. At the present stage the
divisions are operational groupings under a flat org structure —
the Founder directs all three. As the company scales they will
become formal divisional units under Article 10.

**(a) Nyuchi Africa — the parent company.** Corporate, legal,
financial, and strategic functions. The division that signs
contracts, employs people, manages the relationship with the
Foundation, and owns the company's frontier infrastructure assets
(NTL, SiafuDB, Honeycomb protocol). The destination of ultimate
accountability.

**(b) Nyuchi Web Services — the development division.** The
engineering and product organisation. Builds and maintains the
frontier infrastructure. Builds and operates the Mukoko Platform
and the Nyuchi Enterprise products. Conducts paid client services
work. The GitHub organisation
[`nyuchi`](https://github.com/nyuchi) is the public home of this
division.

**(c) Mukoko — the super app division.** Product, growth, content
operations, community management, and business verification for
the Mukoko consumer super app and its sister brands (Zimbabwe
Information Platform, Barstool by Nyuchi). The customer-facing
division.

9.2 The three divisions are operational groupings, not separate
legal entities. All three are parts of Nyuchi Africa (Private)
Limited and share its balance sheet, employees, and governance.
The Foundation is a separate legal entity and is not a division.

### Article 10 — Transition to Formal Divisional Structure

10.1 The company will transition from the flat structure to a
formal divisional structure — with named Division Heads and
delegated decision rights — when any of the following thresholds
is reached:

- **(a)** The company has more than `20` full-time employees.
- **(b)** Any single division generates more than
  `USD 500,000` in annual revenue.
- **(c)** The Mukoko super app reaches `100,000` verified users.
- **(d)** The Founder determines that the flat structure is
  materially impairing execution.

10.2 Upon transition, each division will have a named Division
Head who:

- **(a)** Reports to the Founder & CEO.
- **(b)** Holds delegated authority over the division's roadmap,
  hiring within an approved headcount, and operational decisions
  within the division's budget.
- **(c)** Is accountable for the division's P&L where applicable.
- **(d)** Operates within the decision rights framework of this
  Constitution and any working agreements in effect at the time.

10.3 The transition shall be documented in an amendment to this
Constitution under Article 17. Until such amendment, Article 4.2
governs.

### Article 11 — Client Services within Nyuchi Web Services

11.1 Nyuchi Web Services conducts client services work — custom
development, infrastructure consulting, and platform implementation —
for paying external clients. This is a commercial revenue stream
that sits within the development division.

11.2 Client services work is governed by the following constraints
to protect the integrity of the frontier infrastructure:

- **(a)** Client work is performed under private, proprietary
  terms agreed bilaterally with the client. Client code and client
  data are not open-source and are not mixed with platform or
  infrastructure code.
- **(b)** Code developed specifically for a client belongs to the
  client under the terms of the engagement agreement. Code of
  general utility that emerges from client work may be extracted
  and contributed back to the open infrastructure, subject to
  Article 12 and client consent.
- **(c)** No client engagement may grant the client rights over
  the Mukoko Platform, the Nyuchi Honeycomb protocol, NTL,
  SiafuDB, the canonical documents, or any other core
  infrastructure asset.
- **(d)** The Founder approves client engagements. Engagements
  that present a potential conflict with platform strategy or
  frontier positioning are declined.

11.3 Client services revenue is commingled with general company
revenue. Accounting treats client services as a revenue line item
rather than a separate legal structure.

## Part 4 — Intellectual Property

### Article 12 — Ownership of Work Product

12.1 All work product created by employees and contractors of
Nyuchi Africa in the course of their engagement is the property of
Nyuchi Africa, subject to the exceptions in Article 12.3.

12.2 Employment contracts and contractor agreements shall include
a standard IP assignment clause that vests all work-related
intellectual property in the company, consistent with Zimbabwe law.

12.3 The following are exceptions:

- **(a) Client-owned work.** Work product created specifically for
  a paying client under a client services engagement is owned by
  the client to the extent specified in the engagement agreement.
- **(b) Personal projects.** Work that an employee or contractor
  creates outside working hours, without using company resources,
  and unrelated to the company's business, remains the personal
  property of its author.
- **(c) Upstream contributions.** Contributions to third-party
  open-source projects made in the course of platform or
  infrastructure work are governed by the licence of the upstream
  project. The company makes these contributions freely and does
  not claim ownership over them.

12.4 Contributions from external contributors to
Nyuchi-governed open-source projects are handled under the
Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO) sign-off and a Contributor
Licence Agreement where required, as described in NA-02.

### Article 13 — Licensing Defaults

13.1 The company's default posture is set by NA-02 (Open Source &
Contribution Governance). In summary:

- **(a) Frontier infrastructure** — NTL, SiafuDB, SiafuDB-Kuzu,
  the Honeycomb protocol, service bus event schemas, data layer
  schemas, developer SDKs, and similar primitives — are
  permissively licensed under **Apache 2.0**.
- **(b) Platforms and applications** — the Mukoko consumer super
  app, the Nyuchi Enterprise products, the sister brands, and
  similar product code — are published under an appropriate
  open-source licence (typically **MIT** or **Apache 2.0**, with
  **GPL** used where copyleft is warranted). Some components may
  be private where commercial sensitivity applies, documented in
  NA-02.
- **(c) Client services work** — private and proprietary, owned
  by the client under the terms of the engagement.

13.2 Deviations from these defaults require the Founder's written
approval and are logged in NA-02.

### Article 14 — Trademarks and Brands

14.1 *Nyuchi*, *Nyuchi Africa*, *Nyuchi Web Services*, *Mukoko*,
*Bundu Family*, and related brand marks are trademarks of Nyuchi
Africa. Community use of these marks is governed by a trademark
policy published alongside NA-02.

14.2 The company grants liberal trademark permissions for
community and educational use, and enforces trademark rights only
against uses that create consumer confusion, misrepresent
affiliation, or damage the integrity of the infrastructure.

## Part 5 — People

### Article 15 — Employment and Contracting

15.1 The company hires employees and engages contractors on terms
compliant with Zimbabwe labour law and, where personnel are based
outside Zimbabwe, on terms compliant with the applicable local law.

15.2 The company's hiring practices apply these principles:

- **(a)** Opportunity flows first to Zimbabwean and pan-African
  candidates. Remote-friendly roles remain open to the best
  qualified candidate from any African country.
- **(b)** Compensation is transparent within role bands,
  documented in an internal compensation framework, and reviewed
  annually.
- **(c)** Equity or token allocation for team members, if and
  when established, is governed by a separate instrument approved
  by the Board and consistent with the Foundation's Token
  Governance Policy.
- **(d)** The company invests in capability development —
  training, mentorship, conference attendance — as a core
  obligation, not a perk. Frontier engineering requires continued
  learning and the company funds it.

### Article 16 — Code of Conduct, AI Agents, and Conflicts of Interest

16.1 All employees, contractors, contributors, and representatives
of the company are bound by the **Contributor Covenant 2.1 Code of
Conduct** adopted by the Nyuchi Web Services organisation and
published in the
[`nyuchi/.github`](https://github.com/nyuchi/.github) repository.
The Code of Conduct applies to all company-governed spaces:
codebases, issue trackers, chat channels, events, and public
representations.

16.2 AI-assisted contributions — including contributions made with
the assistance of Claude, Cursor, Copilot, Aider, Devin, Codex, or
similar tools — are governed by the
[`AGENTS.md`](https://github.com/nyuchi/.github/blob/main/AGENTS.md)
policy maintained in `nyuchi/.github`. All AI-assisted work
remains the responsibility of the human who submits it.

16.3 Employees, contractors, and Directors disclose material
conflicts of interest to the Founder in writing. The Founder
maintains a conflicts register and discloses it to the Board at
each Board meeting. The Founder's own concurrent roles in Nyuchi
Africa, the Foundation, and the Family Holding Company are
managed under the framework of the Symbiotic Charter (Clause 2.3)
and disclosed in the Foundation's public governance materials.

## Part 6 — Amendment and Dissolution

### Article 17 — Amendment of this Constitution

17.1 This Constitution may be amended by written decision of the
Founder, subject to the following constraints:

- **(a)** Amendments affecting the Founder's decision rights
  (Article 6), decisions reserved to the Board (Article 7), or
  decisions reserved to the shareholders (Article 8) require
  Board approval by simple majority.
- **(b)** Amendments that would conflict with the Symbiotic
  Charter, the articles of association, or the Platform
  Stewardship Agreement are void.
- **(c)** Amendments transitioning the company to the formal
  divisional structure under Article 10 require Board
  notification but not Board approval.
- **(d)** Amendments reducing the company's open-source
  commitments below the defaults in Article 13 require Board
  approval and consultation with the Mukoko Foundation.

17.2 All amendments are logged in a changelog at the foot of this
document and dated.

### Article 18 — Dissolution

18.1 The dissolution of Nyuchi Africa is a shareholder decision
under Article 8.1(e) and the Symbiotic Charter. In the event of
dissolution:

- **(a)** Client services obligations are settled in accordance
  with their respective engagement agreements.
- **(b)** Open-source infrastructure assets — notably NTL,
  SiafuDB, and the Honeycomb protocol — are transferred to the
  Mukoko Foundation or to a successor organisation acceptable to
  the Foundation, in accordance with the Symbiotic Charter's
  open-source failsafe provisions.
- **(c)** Employee obligations are settled in accordance with
  applicable labour law.
- **(d)** Residual assets are distributed to shareholders in
  accordance with the articles of association.

18.2 The Foundation's authority over the community's sovereign
assets (MIT, MXT, Pod) is unaffected by the dissolution of
Nyuchi Africa.
